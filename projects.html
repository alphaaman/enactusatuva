Alan Wei, Food Waste Minimization

In 2010, 15% of U.S. households described that they were "food insecure", meaning that they consistently reported reduced quality, variability, or desirability of their diet. However, grocery stores, restaurants, and supermarkets throw away perfectly edible food, comprising up to 10% of their total product. Using technology to coordinate logistical efforts to transport food to those in need, the Food Waste Minimization project seeks to build tools to aid both sides of this equation.

Claire D'Alessio, High School Case Competition

The High School Case Competition is a great opportunity to make a difference in a local business, gain experience in working with a business case, and make connections with an outstanding group of students and faculty. The HSCC presents high school students with a business case and asks them to devise recommendations to solve the problem at hand. As a team member of the High School Case Competition you will be in charge of recruiting high schools to participate, preparing materials for the competition, and planning the one day event so it runs smoothly. As this is only the second annual competition, there is room for improvement and your input as a team member is greatly appreciated. If you are looking for an exciting and unique opportunity, join the Enactus High School Case Competition team!

Rahul Kapoor, Tranlin

I’m counting down the days until Enactus at UVA gets the extraordinary opportunity to connect, network with, and help kickstart Virginia’s largest foreign direct investment in history. Tranlin Inc. has the potential to revolutionize the paper and fertilizer industry in our state, and with its $2 billion investment from the Chinese, take a stand against traditional industry’s habitual eco-unfriendliness.
